Event Management in Tourism (1-month intensive course)
Goal
To provide participants with comprehensive knowledge and practical skills in planning, organizing, and
managing events within the tourism and hospitality industry, ensuring successful, sustainable, and
impactful experiences.
Course Purposes / Learning Outcomes
By the end of the course, participants will be able to:
 Understand the principles and processes of event management in tourism.
 Design and plan tourism-related events, considering objectives, target audiences, and budgets.
 Apply marketing and promotional strategies to enhance event visibility and attendance.
 Manage logistics, resources, and stakeholder coordination effectively.
 Identify potential risks and implement safety and sustainability measures.
 Evaluate the success and impact of tourism events for continuous improvement.
Course Plan
Week 1: Introduction &; Foundations
 Overview of tourism and event management
 Types of events in tourism (festivals, cultural, corporate, sports, etc.)
 Event life cycle and planning processes

Week 2: Event Design &; Planning
 Defining objectives and target audience
 Budgeting, scheduling, and resource allocation
 Venue selection, logistics, and supplier management
Week 3: Marketing &; Implementation
 Event promotion and communication strategies
 Coordination of teams and stakeholders
 Risk management, safety, and sustainability considerations
Week 4: Monitoring, Evaluation &amp; Reporting
 Performance indicators and evaluation methods
 Post-event reporting and feedback collection
 Presentation of event proposals and project work
