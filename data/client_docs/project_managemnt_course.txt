Certificate Courses

Project Management in Tourism (1-month intensive course)
Goal
To equip participants with practical and theoretical knowledge of project management principles
specifically applied to the tourism and hospitality industry, enabling them to plan, implement, and
evaluate tourism projects effectively.
Course Purposes/Learning Outcomes
By the end of the course, participants will be able to:
 Understand the fundamental concepts and frameworks of project management.
 Apply project management tools and techniques in tourism contexts.
 Develop and plan tourism projects considering feasibility, budgeting, and resource allocation.
 Identify potential risks and implement effective risk management strategies.
 Monitor and evaluate tourism projects to ensure quality and sustainability.
 Enhance teamwork, leadership, and communication skills within project settings.
Course Plan
Week 1: Introduction &amp; Foundations
 Overview of tourism project management
 Project life cycle and key concepts
 Stakeholder identification and analysis
Week 2: Planning &amp; Design
 Project scope, objectives, and deliverables
 Work breakdown structure (WBS)
 Budgeting, scheduling, and resource management
Week 3: Implementation &amp; Monitoring
 Project execution strategies
 Risk assessment and mitigation in tourism projects
 Performance monitoring and quality assurance
Week 4: Evaluation &amp; Reporting
 Project evaluation methods
 Sustainability and impact assessment in tourism projects
 Presentation of project proposals and final reports
